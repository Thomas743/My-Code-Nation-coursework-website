Unit 2, outcome 2, question 1


Copyright can affect website designers in many ways. For example, if you used a picture without giving credit to the original artist you would be in breach of copyright and are at risk of having your content removed and in extreme cases be sued for profiting of someone else’s work. When using someone’s pictures as background material you can run the risk of having no content displayed of the original owner removes the picture from the website. If you made a website, it is industry practise to put copyright logos on your website so that no one else can use the format of it. If you don’t the copyright infringer could claim you didn’t copyright your website and your claim is invalid.



Unit 2, outcome 2, question 2


There are many disabilities that should be taken into consideration when designing a website. For example, people may be hard of hearing and not be able to listen to a video you are going to upload to your website. This could be easily fixed if you provided a video transcript for each video you have on your website which will allow hard of hearing people to watch your video. Blind people use features that say what your content is instead of reading it. If you write an article that uses pictures without putting information of what the pictures are blind people will not be able to consume your content. In general, it would be good practise to always ask yourself how could someone not consume this information? And provide solutions for these issues in your code.



Unit 2, outcome 2, question 3



There are different factors to consider when choosing what file type, you would like to use for an image on your website. For a simple animation you might want to consider using the GIFF file format. GIFF supports simple animation and is restricted to supporting 256 colours. Alpha would be a suitable choice if you wanted transparency in your picture. JPG is widely considered the best file format as it supports compression which means the file sizes will be smaller than other like the BMP format which doesn’t support compression. For pictures not taken by a camera SVG could be suitable format as the pixels can be scaled infinitely and can lead to better results than other formats.
